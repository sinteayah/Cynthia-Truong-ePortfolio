Life & Work

Hello, I'm Cynthia (she/her), an aspiring librarian passionate about creating meaningful experiences for library patrons. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Bachelor of Arts in 2022 and have been working as a Library Page for the San Jose Public Library system ever since. My days are filled with helping patrons with their information needs, taming towering book carts, and teaming up with amazing staff to keep our library thriving and community engaged.

My journey toward librarianship has definitely been fueled by a wide range of experiences. Before stepping into the world of public libraries, I had the joy of working at a Natural History Museum, where I developed a love for making knowledge accessible and engaging for the public. Later, I volunteered at a local elementary school, where I taught engaging weekly science lessons to 1st through 3rd graders. I also worked as a camp counselor, designing dynamic science curricula for K-6th graders. These experiences helped me embrace my passion for education, creativity, and the pursuit of lifelong learning.

Beyond the library and reading, I'm an art lover (especially ceramics), an avid museum and concert-goer, and an admirer of both cats and marine life. I try to live with curiosity and excitement, always looking for new experiences and finding the joy in the little things. Something that I often tell myself is that we should "take each day in, one day at a time".
