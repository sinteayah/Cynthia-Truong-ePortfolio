Introduction

"It's been my experience that you can nearly always enjoy things if you make up your mind firmly that you will."

- Anne of Green Gables by Lucy Maud Montgomery

In a world teeming with infinite information, libraries stand as quiet sanctuaries—spaces where dignity and equity thrive, and where every individual, regardless of background, is welcomed and valued. We enter seeking something—whether it’s knowledge, solace, or inspiration—and often leave with more than we imagined: new perspectives, deeper understanding, and a sense of connection. Libraries are no longer merely repositories of books; they can be recognized as living bridges that connect diverse stories, ideas, and cultures. Within their walls, there is always a search for meaning, and the possibilities for discovery are limitless.

Life & Work

Hello! I'm Cynthia (she/her), an aspiring librarian with a passion for creating meaningful, inclusive experiences for all individuals. Since earning my Bachelor of Arts from the University of Southern California in 2022, I’ve been working as a Library Page for the San José Public Library, where I support day-to-day operations, assist patrons with their information needs, and collaborate with an amazing team to keep our library thriving and our community engaged.

Before joining the public library world, I had the chance to explore different paths—from working at a Natural History Museum to teaching science to elementary students and designing STEM programs as a camp counselor. These experiences, all rooted in education and curiosity, helped me realize that what I truly love is connecting people with knowledge in meaningful, creative ways—a perfect match for public librarianship.

Outside of work, I’m drawn to all things art and wonder. I’m a fan of ceramics, live music, film photography, cute trinkets, and beautiful animation. I love museums, admire cats and marine life, and will never (and can not) say no to a good plate of pad thai. Whether I’m out and about exploring new ideas or new places, I try to take things in stride—one day at a time!

Organization & Structure

This e-Portfolio serves as a capstone to my MLIS journey at San Jose State University School of Information from August 2023 to May 2025. It demonstrates my mastery of the program’s 14 core competencies and highlights how my academic work, practical experience, and personal values align with the evolving field of library and information science. Each essay explores a competency’s significance to the field and to my own professional development, then presents evidence of my work through coursework, programs, writing, and real-world experiences. I conclude each section with a reflection on how I plan to continue applying the competency in future contexts, always with a focus on equity, creativity, and curiosity.

Without organization, this process wouldn’t have been so streamlined. Since the start of the program, I’ve used Google Drive to archive my coursework—organized by class, semester, and related competency—which made it easy to track my growth and retrieve meaningful work for this portfolio. I continued that structure by creating planning folders for each competency, outlining essays, and refining multiple drafts before finalizing each one. To bring this portfolio to life, I chose to build it with my beginner HTML/CSS skills and host it using GitHub Pages. This platform offered me full creative control over the site’s design and structure, giving me a hands-on, flexible approach. It also gave me a chance to strengthen my digital skillset—which is an increasingly important asset in today’s library and information landscape.

The artifacts featured here span STEAM youth programs, collaborative presentations, research papers, blog posts, and public service experience. Together, they reflect not just the knowledge I’ve gained, but the growth I’ve experienced as an adaptable, collaborative, and community-centered professional.

Of course, this process was not without challenges, but it offered an invaluable chance to reflect, synthesize, and look forward. As I move toward a career in public librarianship, I bring with me the lessons, questions, and relationships that shaped my time in the program—and the excitement to keep learning and contributing, one connection at a time.
