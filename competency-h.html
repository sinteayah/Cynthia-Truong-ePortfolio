Competency H

Demonstrate proficiency in identifying, using, and evaluating current and emerging information and communication technologies.

Introduction

Technological proficiency has become an important competency for information professionals, as rapid technological advancements continue to reshape how information is accessed, consumed, and created. With the proliferation of information and communication technologies (ICT), libraries and other information organizations have redefined their role in the community. Information professionals no longer simply curate physical collections or serve as gatekeepers of knowledge; they have become facilitators of technological access, integrating new tools and creating spaces like makerspaces to build creativity, collaboration, and hands-on learning (Hirsh, 2015; Breeding, 2021). The skillset of information professionals has expanded beyond traditional librarian duties to include specialized technological skills in areas like network management, database administration, website development, and technical support for digital tools (Hirsh, 2015). Keeping up with emerging technologies is essential for enhancing service delivery, keeping libraries relevant in a digital-first world, and helping users navigate the rapidly evolving technological landscape.

In my work at the library, I have been able to directly engage with a range of digital tools and platforms, which has helped me build the technical proficiency needed to excel in today's information environment. For example, public libraries have long relied on integrated library systems (ILS) to manage collections, track circulation, and facilitate user access to resources. The Sierra ILS, used by many libraries, streamlines these processes, and I have familiarity in efficiently managing and troubleshooting cataloging and circulation tasks. By incorporating these practices into my own professional development, I am building a comprehensive understanding of how to identify, use, and evaluate technologies in ways that serve both the institution and the broader community. This skillset not only enhances my own technical capabilities but also prepares me to contribute to the evolving role of information professionals in today's digital landscape.

Identifying

Identifying new technologies requires a proactive and informed approach to spotting emerging trends that could enhance the services and operational efficiencies of information organizations. Libraries are increasingly adopting technologies such as mobile apps, augmented reality, virtual reality, and digital preservation tools to connect with users and improve service delivery (Breeding, 2021). Even with that, however, technological obsolescence remains a major challenge, as rapidly evolving tools can become outdated or unsupported, requiring continuous evaluation and adaptation. The identification process often begins with trend spotting and awareness of technological developments in sectors like business and education, which have a direct impact on libraries (Breeding, 2015). To make sure that these technologies meet the needs of their communities, many information organizations test new tools in controlled environments, such as pilot programs or innovation labs, allowing both the library staff and the users to explore and assess their value before full adoption (Breeding, 2021). This careful approach enables organizations to remain agile and adopt tools that align with their mission and the needs of their community while also planning for long-term sustainability to mitigate obsolescence.

Using

Using technology effectively is a core component of technological proficiency. Information professionals must apply these tools to improve library services, support user engagement, and create innovative learning environments. The use of emerging technologies like makerspaces, mobile apps, coding platforms, and data analytics can help libraries provide new educational and collaborative opportunities for their users (Hirsh, 2015). Professionals need to be well-versed in using these tools, whether for creating digital content, facilitating interactive learning, or improving internal workflows. As libraries increasingly integrate technologies into their services, professionals must make sure that these tools align with the needs of the users and provide meaningful experiences. The ability to use technology not only requires technical expertise but also the ability to understand the role of these tools in enhancing the library's mission and connecting users with relevant information and services.

Evaluating

Evaluating new technologies is a critical step in determining their suitability for adoption and integration into library services. It involves assessing the potential impact of technologies on both internal operations as a staff and user engagement. Information professionals must examine factors like ease of use, compatibility with existing systems, scalability, and the potential for user adoption when evaluating emerging tools such as augmented reality, learning management systems, and mobile apps (Breeding, 2021). This evaluation process also includes considering the technological skills required for effective use and the training necessary for both staff and users. An effective evaluation not only assesses the technical feasibility of the technology but also makes sure that it supports the strategic goals of the library and enhances the user experience. Information professionals are always critically evaluating each tool's ability to meet the evolving needs of the community while contributing to the library’s broader objectives.

Evidence

Group Project: COVID-19 Data Visualization (INFO 246)

My first piece of evidence is a slide presentation and an accompanying infographic from a group project for Information Technology Tools and Applications: Information Visualization (INFO 246). This coursework prepared me to identify, use, and evaluate emerging information technologies by introducing data visualization tools, particularly Tableau. Tasked with gathering and analyzing COVID-19 data, my role in the group focused on creating meaningful visualizations to depict cases and deaths categorized by race and ethnicity in California. Using Tableau for the first time, I developed bar graphs and pie charts with color coding and data labels to enhance clarity and interpretation. These visualizations, showcased both in the slide presentation and the standalone infographic, provided insights into healthcare disparities, highlighting that Latino and White populations had the highest cases and deaths, while Asian, Multi-Race, and Native American groups exhibited distinct vulnerabilities. Through this project, I demonstrated the ability to identify and evaluate emerging technologies by selecting and using the dynamic visualization tool, Tableau, to present complex data in accessible visual formats. The slide presentation contextualized key findings, while the infographic distilled essential insights for broader dissemination. In these deliverables, I am leveraging advanced technologies to support data-driven decision-making and effective information communication.

Assignment #7- Financial Report Generator Using Python (INFO 240)

Your Financial Report:

In INFO 240: Information Technology Tools and Applications-Advanced, I developed essential programming skills, particularly in Python, by working on various projects. One such project was creating a financial report generator, which allowed users to input data like their name, birth date, salary, and work history, and then generated statistics such as the percentage of life spent working and estimated lifetime earnings. This course was my first hands-on experience building an interactive program from scratch, and it deepened my understanding of how technology can be leveraged to turn raw data into valuable insights.

The artifact itself is a Python script that processes user input, applies calculations, and outputs a financial report. I used both VSCode and PyCharm as my integrated development environments (IDEs) for development. In VSCode, I utilized features like syntax highlighting and debugging tools, while in PyCharm, I tested its capabilities to optimize the script. After development, I uploaded the final code to a server for demonstration purposes. The script prompts the user for their data, performs calculations to assess work history and earnings, and then presents the results in a readable, user-friendly format.

This project demonstrates my ability to use current programming tools to create effective information systems. By developing an interactive program that processes and synthesizes personal data into meaningful financial insights, I showcased my ability to leverage emerging technologies for practical applications. The project not only allowed me to apply technical skills in Python but also required me to analyze the data effectively, making sure that the final product was both functional and useful to the user. This experience highlights my competency in utilizing modern technologies to enhance decision-making and automate processes, showcasing a real-world application of technology in the information field.

Digital Curation Case Study Proposal (INFO 284)

One key piece of evidence that showcases my ability to effectively identify, use, and evaluate information technologies is my Digital Curation Case Study proposal from INFO 284 Digital Curation course. In this proposal for a fictional setting, I outline a plan for curating and preserving a large-scale digital dataset, focusing on the technologies and methodologies needed to overcome the unique challenges of digital curation. For instance, I explore the use of cloud services like Amazon Web Services (AWS) for scalable storage and discuss how emerging technologies such as machine learning and natural language processing (NLP) can be leveraged to automate tasks such as metadata creation and content categorization. Additionally, the proposal addresses critical challenges in digital curation, such as technology obsolescence and the need to guarantee long-term data integrity, and outlines how the chosen tools can effectively meet these challenges. Through this proposal, I demonstrated my capacity to evaluate and select appropriate technological solutions for digital curation tasks. By integrating tools like AWS for scalable storage, alongside machine learning and NLP for automating metadata tasks, I showcased both my understanding of current technological trends and how to implement them in digital preservation. This artifact highlights my proficiency in evaluating the impact of new technologies on information environments and selecting solutions that are both effective and sustainable.

e-Portfolio Website Using HTML and CSS (INFO 240)

One of the most personally rewarding projects that demonstrates my ability to work with technologically sophisticated tools is the website I created for my MLIS ePortfolio. This project applied knowledge gained from INFO 240: Information Technology Tools and Applications, a course that introduced key web development principles and provided hands-on experience with coding and website management. While I started with a basic template, I customized all aspects of the site’s design, content formatting, and structure independently. Utilizing HTML and CSS, I experimented with different styling techniques and layouts, deepening my ability to manipulate code to create an aesthetically cohesive and functional digital space. Publishing the site through GitHub provided additional experience with cloud-based hosting and version control, allowing me to understand how digital platforms can be managed and shared effectively. Beyond basic coding, I had to learn how to integrate various web technologies to enhance the overall functionality and usability for the first time. I incorporated JavaScript for interactive elements, PHP for reusable components that allowed global updates, and XML for structured data management. Additionally, I structured the site’s backend with organized directories for images, stylesheets, and scripts, improving maintainability. On the front end, I applied some responsive web design (RWD) principles to make sure there is seamless access and usability across devices.

Conclusion

The evolving landscape of Library and Information Science (LIS) is being shaped by the rapid advancement of new technologies, fundamentally altering how we access, organize, and share information. While my career focus may lean toward public librarianship, where I may not directly interact with all of these technologies, I believe that it is still crucial to expand my knowledge and understanding of emerging tools. The beneftis of staying informed about these developments allows me to stay flexible and prepared to meet the ever-changing needs of library users. By embracing technological proficiency, I can not only enhance my own professional growth but also hopefully contribute meaningfully to a future-oriented, innovative library environment.
